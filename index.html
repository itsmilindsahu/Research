Abstract

The Core Idea

Credit default prediction is usually framed as a binary classification problem, but this framing ignores the timing of default and discards information from censored loans that are still active at the observation cutoff.

Survival analysis offers a more natural formulation by treating default and prepayment as time-to-event processes with explicit handling of censoring. This paper proposes applying reliability-theoretic structure — specifically cohort-level hazard ordering and mean residual lifetime estimation — as additional modeling constraints within machine learning survival models for fintech credit risk.

The core question is whether incorporating such constraints improves calibration, temporal stability, and cohort-level consistency compared with otherwise equivalent unconstrained survival models.
